About LUWI Foundation

LUWI Foundation is a student-centered organization committed to creating safe, empowering, and inclusive learning environments for pupils and students in Zambia.

We support learners facing academic pressure, mental health challenges, financial hardship, gender-based violence (GBV), menstrual hygiene challenges, disability barriers, early marriages, unwanted pregnancies, and exploitation.

Our Story

LUWI Foundation was born from personal struggle, resilience, and purpose. After excelling in Grade 12, the founder enrolled at university under a 100% government bursary scholarship. However, he was placed in a program that did not align with his passion.

Facing emotional and academic challenges, he later pursued a Bachelor of Science in Nursing. Despite severe financial hardship — surviving on limited upkeep — he persevered.

In his second year, a vision was born: to create a safe environment where no student feels unheard, unsupported, or forced to bury their talents.

The name “LUWI” came from a dream and was later discovered to mean “God’s Mercy.” LUWI exists to be mercy, hope, and light for students stuck in darkness.
